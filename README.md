## 💡 Overview

<a href="#dxOFilmPack">DxO FilmPack</a> serves photographers who want to achieve the aesthetic qualities of analog film in their digital work — fine art photographers seeking the specific color character of classic film stocks, portrait photographers who prefer the skin tone rendering of certain films, street photographers who want the gritty black-and-white qualities of classic photojournalism films, and anyone who finds the film aesthetic more pleasing than digital color.

The <a href="#dxOFilmPack">fine art photography use case</a> applies specific film stocks for their aesthetic qualities — Velvia for landscape saturation, Portra for portrait color warmth, Fuji Neopan for black-and-white tonality — creating images with the characteristic look of shooting on those films without the cost, processing complexity, and unpredictability of actually shooting film. The <a href="#dxOFilmPack">portrait photography use case</a> uses film emulations for the skin tone rendering qualities that certain film stocks are known for — Kodak Portra's reputation for flattering skin tones in portrait photography translates to the FilmPack emulation.

The <a href="#dxOFilmPack">photojournalism aesthetic use case</a> applies classic black-and-white film simulations — Ilford HP5, Kodak Tri-X, Kodak T-MAX — for the high-contrast, gritty grain character that defines the photojournalism tradition. <a href="#dxOFilmPack">Instagram and social media aesthetic use case</a> applies vintage and film-inspired presets as a one-click creative treatment for photographers who want a consistent aesthetic across their social media photography.</p>

<a href="#dxOFilmPack">Color rendering customization</a> adjusts the color rendering component of the film simulation independently — pushing the color cast toward or away from the film's characteristic rendering for creative latitude beyond pure film reproduction. <a href="#dxOFilmPack">Preset saving and management</a> saves custom combinations of film emulation, grain settings, and creative effects as named presets — building a personal look library for consistent application across work.

<a href="#dxOFilmPack">Essential and Elite editions</a> provide different library sizes — Essential with a curated selection of key film stocks and Elite with the complete 80-plus stock library and all creative effects. <a href="#dxOFilmPack">DxO's optical science heritage</a> — a company whose primary expertise is lens and sensor characterization — applies the same measurement rigor to film emulation that DxO applies to optical correction.

## 🤔 FAQ

<details>
<summary>What creative effects does FilmPack include beyond film emulations?</summary>

Light leaks, vignettes, borders, dust and scratch textures, faded print effects, and other vintage and creative treatments.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I adjust how strongly the film emulation applies?</summary>

Yes. Rendering intensity and individual component controls adjust the balance between the digital original and the film treatment.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does FilmPack handle RAW files?</summary>

FilmPack processes developed images. RAW processing happens in the host application (Lightroom, PhotoLab) or as a standalone workflow with compatible files.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What film grain rendering does FilmPack provide?</summary>

Grain rendering reproduces film-specific grain size, distribution, and statistical properties — differentiated between different film stocks and exposure values.

</details>

Check the current DxO website for available licensing options as DxO has offered both perpetual and subscription models.
